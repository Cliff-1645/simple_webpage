        <section id="farming-systems">
            <h2>Farming Systems</h2>
            <button class="accordion">Extensive System</button>
            <div class="panel">
                <p>It involves aquatic organisms farmed in enclosed structures placed within habitats.</p>
                <p><strong>Examples:</strong> catfish, oysters, tilapia</p>
                <h3>Advantage</h3>
                <ul>
                    <li>It makes it possible to selectively increase the production of species for man's food.</li>
                </ul>
                <h3>Disadvantages</h3>
                <ul>
                    <li>Causes eutrophication</li>
                    <li>Disease spread</li>
                    <li>Invasive species</li>
                </ul>
            </div>

            <button class="accordion">Semi-Intensive System</button>
            <div class="panel">
                <p>It combines natural food sources with supplementary feeding to enhance fish production.</p>
                <p><strong>Examples:</strong> prawns, shrimps, tilapia</p>
                <h3>Advantages</h3>
                <ul>
                    <li>Provides protection for fishes.</li>
                    <li>Equipment needed is inexpensive.</li>
                </ul>
                <h3>Disadvantages</h3>
                <ul>
                    <li>Lower productivity.</li>
                    <li>Higher infrastructure cost.</li>
                </ul>
            </div>

            <button class="accordion">Intensive System</button>
            <div class="panel">
                <p>Highly controlled fish farming where maximum production of fish is achieved from a minimum quantity of water.</p>
                <h3>Advantages</h3>
                <ul>
                    <li>Produces more fish since there is higher productivity.</li>
                    <li>Efficient resource use.</li>
                </ul>
                <h3>Disadvantages</h3>
                <ul>
                    <li>Water pollution</li>
                    <li>Can cause eutrophication.</li>
                </ul>
            </div>
        </section>

        <section id="health">
            <h2>Health</h2>
            <h3>Diseases</h3>
            <button class="accordion">Mouth Fungus Disease</button>
            <div class="panel">
                <p><strong>Affects:</strong> Tilapia</p>
                <p><strong>Symptom:</strong> White cottony patches around the mouth</p>
                <p><strong>Cause:</strong> Bacterium chondrococcus columnaris</p>
                <p><strong>Treatment:</strong> Penicillin at 10000 units per litre is effective.</p>
            </div>

            <button class="accordion">Tuberculosis</button>
            <div class="panel">
                <p><strong>Affects:</strong> Catfish</p>
                <p><strong>Symptoms:</strong> Hollow belly, Sores</p>
                <p><strong>Cause:</strong> Bacterium mycobacterium piscium</p>
                <p><strong>Treatment:</strong> Since there is no treatment, destroy the infected fish and keep away from the other species.</p>
            </div>

            <button class="accordion">Ulcer</button>
            <div class="panel">
                <p><strong>Affects:</strong> Mudfish, Tilapia</p>
                <p><strong>Symptoms:</strong> Slow body movement, Loss of appetite</p>
                <p><strong>Cause:</strong> Bacteria haemophilus</p>
                <p><strong>Treatment:</strong> Use antibiotics oxytetracycline and chloramphenicol.</p>
            </div>
        </section>

                    panel.style.maxHeight = null;
                } else {

                }
            });

                });
            });
